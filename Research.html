Research

Primary Reseach

The homepage of Hilton Pond Center's website (above) is a picture-perfect look into the kind of design and branding that a viewer will find throughout the website. On this page there are bright colors, pixelated images, and the use of almost entirely Comic Sans, much light every other page in the website with changes to color only.

Significant Pages

This image is a screenshot of Hilton Pond Center's main page, just after the header information and navigation. It shows the important news for the week, current updates, and button for their weekly newsletter that points to a different box about humming birds. Everything commands attention and equal importance because everything is brightly colored or bolded in some way. This is pretty typically information one might find on a homepage, however it is very hard to visually sift through to find what you are looking for.

This screenshot is of the newsletter sign-up page. When you click "Weekly Happenings" on the homepage, it takes you to the newletter sign up page, not the actual newsletter. The newsletter is located on a different page entirely and I only found that by accident. This page includes a different logo that could be considered to be on brand with the rest of the website, however becuase of the style of the image used for the logo, it looks as though it was found on clip art in outdated versions of PowerPoint. Not to mention that everything is noticeably pixelated, making the overall design of the website feel haphazard and somewhat unprofessional. The content itself is the saving grace of the website.

Images associated with Hilton Pond Center

These images came up when searching "Hilton Pond Center" into Google, save for the image on the bottom right. That is a screenshot taken from the publications page. It shows a completely different color pallet and design than the other pages and was worth mentioning in this stage of the research. They can either be found on Hilton Pond's website or their Facebook page. I chose to include these images in my research because they is little to no branding for this organization and any image associated with them is an close as I was going to get as far as consistent branding was concerned.

Secondary Research

These images were taken as part of the secondary research.
